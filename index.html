They develop gradually through prolonged static work and too little variation during the day.

Most employees do not notice the strain while working. They often only recognize it after weeks or months of repeating the same patterns.

The issue is rarely intensity. It is duration. Human Load helps organizations recognize these patterns early and introduce preventive work habits before complaints become persistent.

While ergonomics focuses on the workstation, we focus on what happens within it — the daily behaviors, rhythms, and habits that either protect employees or gradually wear them down.

A 12-week trajectory built around daily reality.

Every trajectory starts with a structured intake to understand workload patterns and existing risk factors. The goal is not temporary relief — but lasting behavioral change.

1 / 6
01 Week 1-2

Awareness

Employees discover how physical load can develop during concentrated screen work and why static posture accumulates over time.

02 Week 3-4

Static Load

The program explains why static muscle activation creates risk over time — and how to interrupt the pattern before it builds.

03 Week 5-6

Work Rhythm

Participants learn how work rhythm influences recovery and physical strain — and begin to experiment with micro-breaks.

04 Week 7-8

Early Signals

Employees learn to recognize subtle warning signals before complaints develop — building a new kind of body awareness at work.

05 Week 9-10

Micro Variation

Small posture changes and brief resets reduce continuous physical load throughout the workday without disrupting productivity.

06 Week 11-12

Prevention Embedded

Preventive behavior becomes integrated into everyday work routines. Teams are equipped to continue independently after the trajectory ends.

FAQ

Clarity for HR
and leadership teams.

What makes Human Load different from ergonomic assessments?

Human Load focuses on behavior in daily work routines, not only on setup. The emphasis is on prevention, variation, and lasting behavioral change — not a one-off workstation adjustment.

How much time does the program require from employees?

The trajectory is designed to be practical and manageable. Short modules and low-threshold exercises fit into the existing workday without requiring significant disruption.

Is the program suitable for remote or hybrid teams?

Yes. The approach can be applied in office, hybrid, and remote work environments as long as screen-based work is part of the daily routine.

How do you measure the impact of the program?

We look at workload patterns, behavioral adoption, practical implementation, and the extent to which preventive routines are embedded in daily work.

What happens after the 12-week trajectory?

By the end of the program, teams should be able to continue preventive behavior more independently — the goal is self-sufficiency, not dependency.
