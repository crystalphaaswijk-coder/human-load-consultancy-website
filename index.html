Physical Load in Screen-Based Work

Complaints rarely appear overnight.

They develop gradually through prolonged static work and lack of variation during the day.

Most employees do not notice the strain while working — they only feel it after weeks or months.

The issue is rarely intensity. It is duration.

Human Load helps organizations recognize these patterns early and introduce preventive work habits before complaints develop.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clarity for HR and leadership teams

What makes Human Load different from ergonomic assessments?

Human Load focuses on behavior in daily work routines, not only on setup. The emphasis is on prevention, variation, and lasting behavioral change.

How much time does the program require from employees?

The trajectory is designed to be practical and manageable, with short modules and low-threshold exercises that fit into the workday.

Is the program suitable for remote or hybrid teams?

Yes. The approach can be applied in office, hybrid, and remote work environments as long as screen-based work is part of the daily routine.

How do you measure the impact of the program?

We look at workload patterns, behavioral adoption, practical implementation, and the extent to which preventive routines are embedded in daily work.

What happens after the 12-week trajectory?

By the end of the program, teams should be able to continue preventive behavior more independently, with routines that remain usable after the trajectory ends.
